# `HACKER GUIDE - Mistral AI Worldwide Hackathon` 

`First of all, congratulations on your selection ! We received over 7,000 applications for this hackathon, but you made it !` 

`This guide will hopefully tell you all you need to know to make this one of the best hackathons you’ve ever participated in.`

`Before we jump in, we just wanted to thank you for your participation. A successful hackathon relies on all of you building like crazy over the weekend, and we can’t wait to see what you’ll be able to build.`

1. ## `Practical information`

**`Important links`**

- `Discord: https://discord.gg/zdSEmdfkSQ IMPORTANT : you must go into the “Roles” tab at the top (below events) and click “Yes” on “Are you participating in a Mistral AI hackathon”. This will add you to the hackathon channel, where you can go into “your-location” channel and choose your location.`

`Everything will happen from 9 AM (local time to each city) on the 28th of February to around 7 PM (local time to each city) on the 1st of March, except online which will have an extended deadline.`

`If you participate in a city, you are expected to be at the venue during the hackathon (all addresses are on luma), except overnight.`

2. ## `Rules & Tracks`

`Teams must consist of 1 to 4 people.`  
`We encourage you to not work solo, as teamwork is an integral part of the hackathon experience.`

`If you have trouble finding teammates, have a look on Discord where you’ll find an entire channel dedicated just to that.`

`We offer three tracks as part of this hackathon:`

1. **`Mistral AI track`**

`Build anything with the Mistral API.`  
`Create agents, tools, products, experiments… no constraints, just ship something ambitious, creative, and impactful.`

2. **`Fine-tuning by Weights & Biases`**

`This track is for technically strong builders who want to fine-tune Ministral, Mistral Small, Mistral Medium, Codestral, or other Mistral models on a specific task of their choosing.`

`You’ll find more information in the last section of this document.`

3. **`On-device by Nvidia (for Paris, London, NYC only)`**

`NVIDIA hosts the On-Device Track for builders who want to deploy Mistral models directly on edge and local hardware. This track focuses on optimizing, accelerating, and running models efficiently on-device.`

4. **`Challenges`**

`Besides tracks, there are also global and local challenges. You must compete in a track, and you can compete in a challenge.`

1. `Global challenges`

- `Hugging Face -> best use of agent skills`  
- `ElevenLabs -> best use of elevenlabs`  
- `Supercell -> best video game project`  
- `Mistral -> best vibe usage`  
    
2. `Local challenges`

- `San Francisco -> WhiteCircle (detailed in “Resources”) and Tilde Research (Best Architectural Modification)`  
- `NYC -> Tilde Research (Best Architectural Modification)`  
- `Paris -> Raise & WhiteCircle`  
- `London -> Jump Trading (Best use of data) & Giant`

3. ## `Submission & Judging`

`You must submit your project before the deadline on the hackiterate.com platform. There will be a live countdown indicating how much time is left. Submission fields include:`

1. `Title / description / project screenshot`  
2. `Track and optionally challenge you enter`  
3. `Github URL`  
4. `Video demo URL (upload to youtube or loom) - 2 MIN MAX.`

`For the participants in cities, judging will happen with you presenting your project in front of the jury live.`

`The judges will grade your project and will use the following five grading criteria:`

1. `Technicality`  
   1. `How well is the system architected? Is the technical implementation solid and modular?`  
   2. `Did the team go beyond simple prompting or one-shot tools — and build an actual sophisticated system (e.g. agents with autonomy, planning, memory, tool use, model fine-tuning…)?`	  
2. `Creativity`  
   1. `How original and inventive is the idea?`  
   2. `Is the concept surprising, playful, or particularly clever?`  
3. `Usefulness`  
   1. `Does the project solve a meaningful or interesting problem?`  
   2. `Can we imagine real users benefiting from it in the near term?`  
4. `Demo`  
   1. `Was the project clearly explained and demoed?`  
   2. `Was the demo functional, showing results in action?`  
   3. `Did the team communicate both the technical architecture and the overall purpose or narrative?`  
5. `Alignment with track`  
   1. `Does the project directly address one of the proposed tracks or challenge themes?`  
   2. `Does the project push the boundaries or offer a new perspective within its chosen challenge area?`

`This will give rise to finalists, who will then present their projects in front of the crowd from that city. Using the live presentation, judges will make their choice for the local winners.`

`The final overall winner will be selected amongst the finalists and a few special projects during a livestream on the 9th March.`
